Dissolved and capillary-trapped mass#

Conserved mass#

From the initial conditions, the initial masses are

\[\begin{split} \begin{align*} m_C^{0}&=\frac{1}{\varepsilon}\int_\Omega \varphi s_0~\text{d}\Omega \\ m_D^{0}&=\int_\Omega \varphi(1-s_0)c_0~\text{d}\Omega \end{align*} \end{split}\]

If \(\varphi=1\), \(s_0 = s_r\text{H}(y-\zeta_0X) + \mathcal{N}_s\) and \(c_0 = c_r\text{H}(y-\zeta_0X)+ \mathcal{N}_c\) on the domain \(\Omega = [0, \mathcal{A}X] \times [0, X]\), then

\[\begin{split} \begin{align*} m_C^0 &= \frac{1}{\varepsilon}\mathcal{A}X^2(1-\zeta_0)\left(s_r + \mathcal{O}(\mathcal{N}_s)\right) + \frac{1}{\varepsilon}\mathcal{A}X^2\zeta_0\mathcal{O}(\mathcal{N}_s)\\ m_D^0 &= \mathcal{A}X^2(1-\zeta_0)\left((1-s_r - \mathcal{O}(\mathcal{N}_s))(c_r + \mathcal{O}(\mathcal{N}_c))\right) + \mathcal{A}X^2\zeta_0(1-\mathcal{O}(\mathcal{N}_s))\mathcal{O}(\mathcal{N}_c) \end{align*} \end{split}\]

If \(\mathcal{O}(\mathcal{N}_s), \mathcal{O}(\mathcal{N}_c) \ll 1\), then approximately

\[\begin{split} \begin{align*} m_C^0 &= \frac{1}{\varepsilon}\mathcal{A}X^2(1-\zeta_0)s_r\\ m_D^0 &= \mathcal{A}X^2(1-\zeta_0)(1-s_r)c_r \\ \end{align*} \end{split}\]

so the total conserved mass is

\[ m_0 = \mathcal{A}X^2(1-\zeta_0)\left(\frac{s_r}{\varepsilon}+(1-s_r)c_r\right) \]

Complete dissolution#

\(c\to c_\infty(\textbf{x})\) and \(s\to0\) as \(t\to\infty\)

\[\begin{split} \begin{align*} m_C^{\infty} & = 0 \\ m_D^{\infty} & = \int_\Omega \varphi c_\infty~\text{d}\Omega \end{align*} \end{split}\]

If \(\varphi=1\), then \(m_D^{\infty}=\text{vol}(\Omega)\langle c_\infty\rangle\) so by mass conservation on \(\Omega = [0, \mathcal{A}X] \times [0, X]\)

\[(1-\zeta_0)\left(\frac{s_r}{\varepsilon}+(1-s_r)c_r\right)=\langle c_\infty\rangle\]

Due to the physical constraint \(\langle c_\infty\rangle\leq 1\) the condition on complete dissolution is obtained as

\[s_r\leq \frac{\varepsilon\left(\frac{1}{1-\zeta_0}-c_r\right)}{1-\varepsilon c_r}\equiv s_r^*\]

Incomplete dissolution#

\(c\to 1\) and \(s\to s_\infty(\textbf{x})\) as \(t\to\infty\)

\[\begin{split} \begin{align*} m_C^{\infty} & = \frac{1}{\varepsilon}\int_\Omega \varphi s_\infty~\text{d}\Omega \\ m_D^{\infty} & = \int_\Omega \varphi(1 - s_\infty)~\text{d}\Omega = \text{vol}(\Omega)\langle\varphi\rangle -\varepsilon m_C^{\infty} \end{align*} \end{split}\]

By mass conservation \(m=m_C^{\infty}+m_D^{\infty}\) so

\[\begin{split} \begin{align*} m_C^{\infty} &= \frac{m_0 - \text{vol}(\Omega)\langle\varphi\rangle}{1-\varepsilon} \\ m_D^{\infty} &= \frac{m_0 - \frac{\text{vol}(\Omega)\langle\varphi\rangle}{\varepsilon}}{1-\frac{1}{\varepsilon}} \end{align*} \end{split}\]

If \(\varphi=1\) and \(\Omega = [0, \mathcal{A}X] \times [0, X]\), then \(\langle\varphi\rangle=1\) and \(\text{vol}(\Omega)=\mathcal{A}X^2\).

Using the previous expression for the total conserved mass and noting the physical constraint \(m_C^{\infty}\geq0\) obtains the same expression for the critical residual saturation \(s_r^*\).
